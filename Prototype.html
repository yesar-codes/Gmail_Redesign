Personas — FIS Capstone: Gmail Redesign

Based on 11 semi-structured interviews (KIT, May 2026). Primary and secondary personas are design targets. Negative personas define who this redesign is explicitly not optimised for — they act as guardrails against over-simplification or over-complexity.

✓ Primary Persona — Design Target

Theo Schmidt

24 · Student & Research Assistant · KIT

Goals: Keep track of study and personal emails; avoid missing important messages.

Behaviour: Reads on mobile, writes on desktop. Uses stars and unread state as informal reminders. Has never used labels or filters.

Frustrations: Loses overview when many emails arrive; hard to tell which still need a reply.

Core need: Important and follow-up emails stay visible without any manual setup.

✓ Secondary Persona — Design Target

Mathilda Bauer

46 · Professional Gardener

Goals: Use Gmail for account messages without spending time on inbox management.

Behaviour: Checks Gmail 1–2× per week. Leaves all emails in inbox. Uses search when needed.

Frustrations: Inbox cluttered with promotions. Older emails require multiple search attempts.

Core need: Recognise and find relevant emails quickly without configuring anything.

✗ Negative Persona — Not our target

David Hartmann

32 · Software Engineer · Power User

Profile: Manages 200+ emails daily. Uses keyboard shortcuts, custom filters, nested labels, and third-party integrations (Zapier, Superhuman). Practices zero-inbox discipline.

Why excluded: The redesign's auto-sorting and simplified defaults would override his carefully tuned workflow. The "Needs Attention" section is redundant for him — he already has a system. Designing for David would push us toward exposing complex configuration panels that Theo would find overwhelming.

Design guardrail: Don't hide advanced features entirely — keep labels/filters accessible but non-mandatory.

✗ Negative Persona — Not our target

Emma Weber

19 · High School Student · Near-Zero Email User

Profile: Opens Gmail fewer than twice a month, only for school registration confirmations. All real communication happens via Instagram and WhatsApp.

Why excluded: No inbox redesign changes her email habits — the medium is irrelevant to her. Follow-up flags, read receipts, and smart search add zero value when you send fewer than 5 emails per month.

Design guardrail: Don't strip the interface to a single button. Frequent users (Theo, Mathilda) need depth — radical simplification would break their workflows.

Design implication: David and Emma sit at opposite extremes of email engagement. Our redesign targets the aware but passive organiser in between — someone who uses Gmail daily but relies on the simplest possible strategies (stars, unread state, search). The negative personas prevent us from drifting toward either extreme: adding power-user configuration panels (for David) or removing all structure (for Emma).
